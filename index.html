Overview

What happens when a dancing body and soft robotics begin to think together?

The Sensorium Project treats the dancing body as both instrument and sensor array.

Working with soft fluidic pressure sensors, micro-vibration elements, and wearable interfaces, we ask how movement, pressure, and touch can be translated into light, sound, and texture without flattening the complexity of feeling.

Rather than chasing emotion labels like "happy" or "sad," this work focuses on affective fields: the intensities, weights, and textures that pass through the body as it moves.

System Architecture

The ACCS: How the System Thinks

01 Hardware Layer

Soft fluidic pressure sensors embedded in silicone sleeves, micro-vibration elements, and wearable rings register changes in pressure, bend, speed, and orientation. The body becomes a multi-channel transmitter.

02 Signal Processing

Raw signals are cleaned and transformed into features like intensity, stability, and fluctuation. The system learns to read qualities: turbulent or steady, compressed or expanded, gripping or releasing.

03 Affective Atmosphere

Processed signals drive the visual and sonic environment: shifting light temperatures, projection dynamics, spatial audio. Like dancing inside a weather system you help create.
